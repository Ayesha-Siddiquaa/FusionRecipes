Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular Indian street food dish consisting of a spicy vegetable curry (bhaji) served with buttered bread rolls (pav). The bhaji is made from a mix of mashed vegetables such as potatoes, tomatoes, cauliflower, peas, and capsicum, cooked with aromatic spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, and red chili powder. It is typically garnished with chopped onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice. The soft and buttery pav is toasted on a griddle until crispy and golden brown, then served alongside the flavorful bhaji. This hearty and satisfying dish is loved by people of all ages and is perfect for any occasion.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : veg

instructions

Preparation

Boil potatoes, cauliflower, peas, and any other desired vegetables until tender. Drain and set aside.

Mashing Vegetables

In a large pan, heat oil and add chopped onions. Saut√© until translucent, then add minced garlic and ginger. Cook until fragrant.

Adding Spices

Add chopped tomatoes to the pan and cook until they break down and become soft. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and pav bhaji masala. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.

Adjusting Consistency

Add water as needed to achieve the desired consistency. The bhaji should be thick and creamy.

Toasting Pav

In another pan, slice the pav rolls horizontally and spread butter on each side. Toast them on a hot griddle until they are crispy and golden brown.

Serving

Serve the hot pav bhaji with a dollop of butter, chopped onions, cilantro leaves, and a wedge of lime. Enjoy it with the toasted pav for a delicious and satisfying meal.

ingredients

Vegetables:

Potatoes, cauliflower, green peas, onion, tomatoes, bell pepper, green chilies, ginger, garlic.

Spices and Seasonings:

Pav Bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin seeds, salt, oil or butters

For Serving:

pav (dinner rolls), butter for toasting pav, coriander leaves, lemon wedges.
