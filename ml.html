Motichur Laddu

Motichur laddu is a popular Indian sweet delicacy made from fine, tiny balls or droplets of besan (gram flour) batter, which are deep-fried until golden brown and then soaked in sugar syrup. These sweet balls are then shaped into laddus, resulting in a delightful treat loved by many. The name "motichur" literally translates to "crushed pearls," aptly describing the small, spherical shape of the laddus resembling pearls. This sweet is often served on festive occasions, celebrations, and religious ceremonies, delighting taste buds with its rich, sweet flavor and melt-in-your-mouth texture.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Veg Dessert

instructions

Prepare Besan Batter:

Mix gram flour (besan) with water to form a thick, smooth batter.

Make Tiny Droplets:

Pour the batter through a perforated ladle or sieve into hot oil, forming tiny droplets that resemble pearls. Fry until golden brown.

Prepare Sugar Syrup:

In a separate pan, heat sugar and water to make a sugar syrup of one-string consistency. Add saffron strands or cardamom powder for flavor if desired.

Soak the Droplets:

Once fried, transfer the tiny fried droplets (boondi) into the sugar syrup. Let them soak for a few minutes to absorb the sweetness.

Shape into Laddus:

After soaking, drain excess syrup and allow the boondi to cool slightly. Then, shape them into round laddus by pressing and rolling them gently between your palms.

Garnish and Serve:

Garnish the laddus with chopped nuts like almonds, pistachios, or cashews for added flavor and decoration. Your delicious motichur laddus are ready to be served and enjoyed!

ingredients

Gram flour (besan)

Sugar

Water

Saffron strands or cardamom powder (optional)

Ghee or oil for frying

Chopped nuts like almonds, pistachios, or cashews for garnish (optional)
